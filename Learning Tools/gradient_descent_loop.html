This is the whole CL / SMO loop. Each iteration: (1) the black-box optics prints the current mask under the current source; (2) we score it against the target with a differentiable loss, the mean-squared error L = mean( (print − target)² ) over every pixel; (3) we ask the gradient ∂L/∂(mask, source) which direction lowers L; (4) we take a small step that way — the mask step nudges pixel transmissions (this is OPC / ILT), the source step reshapes the DOE weights. Repeat, and the loss descends. The bowl on the right is the same story geometrically: the design sits on a loss landscape and rolls toward the valley; the learning rate is how big each roll is — too big and it bounces off the walls, too small and it crawls. Small non-monotonic wiggles are normal (fixed step size, no line search). A soft sigmoid resist makes the print differentiable so a gradient even exists; the sharp edge-error metric is just reported alongside. That's the engine driving the SMO Studio — the optics is exactly the FFT loop from the double-slit demo, here treated as a black box. Physics and landscape are exaggerated for intuition, not drawn to scale.
